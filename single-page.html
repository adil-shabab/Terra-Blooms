It is also called devil's vine or devil's ivy because it is almost impossible to kill and it stays green even when kept in the dark. It is sometimes mistakenly labeled as a Philodendron, Pothos or Scindapsus in plant stores. It is commonly known as a money plant in many parts of the Indian subcontinent

What is Devil's Ivy good for?

Pothos featured in NASA's Clean Air Study, and was proven to remove indoor pollutants such as formaldehyde, trichloroethene, toluene, xylene, and benzene. Many of these are found in common cleaning products we use in our homes.

Sometimes mistakenly labelled as a philodendron in nurseries, it fights nasty toxins like benzene (found in paints and varnishes), xylene and trichloroethylene (found in solvents), and formaldehyde (found in carpets and upholstery), which is why it's a great indoor plant for air purification.
